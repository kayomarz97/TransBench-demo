## Short description (the blurb)
TransBench turns a clinician's bedside observation into a grounded, testable, bench-ready experiment.

You write one plain sentence — *a patient who won't respond to a first-line drug, a lab value that doesn't fit* — and TransBench breaks it into biological angles, writes hypotheses you could prove wrong, and grounds every one in **real, clickable papers** (PubMed + Europe PMC). If a citation won't resolve, it's dropped. Eight agents and three rigor gates, wired together with LangGraph, hand you a bench-ready brief **and** a ready-made prompt for **Claude Science** — which pulls a real, public single-cell dataset, runs the analysis, and draws the figures. A sentence at the bedside → a grounded hypothesis → a figure → a pipette, not another meeting. And it replays **byte-identical**, so the results are yours to reproduce.

Shipped as an **MCP connector for Claude Science**.

*And the film itself?* Script, animation, voiceover timing, and the entire edit were planned and produced by **Claude Code**. I'm a physician — I can't edit video. I just told Claude how I was feeling.
